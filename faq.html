üîê General Questions

What is AegisPix?

AegisPix is a free, user-side tool that lets you password-protect your images using military-grade AES-256 encryption. All processing happens in your browser - your images never leave your device.

Is it really free?

Yes! AegisPix is completely free to use. There are no hidden costs, subscriptions, or premium features. We believe privacy tools should be accessible to everyone.

Do I need to create an account?

No accounts required! ImageSecure works entirely independent from the server. You don't need to register, provide personal information, only need an internet connection for the core encryption/decryption features.

Is my data safe?

Absolutely. Your images are encrypted and decrypted locally in your browser using the Web Crypto API. We never see, store, or transmit your images or passwords. Everything stays on your device.

üîí Encryption Questions

What encryption does AegisPix use?

We use AES-256-GCM encryption, which is the same standard used by governments and military organizations worldwide. This provides both confidentiality (keeping your data secret) and authenticity (ensuring data integrity).

How strong is the encryption?

AES-256 has 2^256 possible key combinations - that's more possible keys than there are atoms in the observable universe. Combined with PBKDF2 key derivation (100,000 iterations), it provides excellent protection against brute-force attacks.

What file formats are supported?

AegisPix works with all common image formats: JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP, and BMP. The encrypted files are saved as .pwimg files, which can only be opened with AegisPix.

Can I encrypt multiple images at once?

Currently, you can only encrypt one image at a time through the web interface. However, you can encrypt multiple images by using the tool repeatedly. The desktop application may support batch processing in the future.

What happens if I forget my password?

If you forget your password, there's no way to recover it. The encryption is designed to be unbreakable without the correct password. Always use strong, memorable passwords and consider keeping a secure backup of important passwords.

üåê Technical Questions

Which browsers are supported?

AegisPix works with all modern browsers that support the Web Crypto API: Chrome 37+, Firefox 34+, Safari 11+, and Edge 79+. For the best experience, use the latest version of your browser.

Is there a file size limit?

The main limitation is your browser's memory. Most browsers can handle images up to 50-100 MB. For very large images, consider using the desktop application or splitting large files.

Can I use this on mobile devices?

Yes,AegisPix works on mobile browsers that support the Web Crypto API. However, the experience is optimized for desktop use. File selection and management is easier on desktop computers.

üîì Decryption Questions

How do I open encrypted images?

Use the "Decrypt Image" tool on our website, simply double-click the .pwimg file. Enter your password, and your image will be displayed or downloaded.

Can I share encrypted images with others?

Yes! You can safely share .pwimg files through email, cloud storage, or any file sharing service. Just share the password separately through a secure channel (not the same as the file).

What if the decryption fails?

Decryption will fail if: 1) The wrong password is entered, 2) The file is corrupted, or 3) The file isn't a valid .pwimg file. Always verify your password and ensure the file wasn't damaged during transfer.

How do you ensure my privacy?

We don't collect any personal information, IP addresses, or usage data. All encryption happens in your browser using industry-standard APIs. Your images and passwords never leave your device.

Is the encryption audited?

AegisPix uses well-established, audited encryption standards (AES-GCM, PBKDF2) implemented through the browser's Web Crypto API. These are the same standards used by major tech companies and government agencies.

What if someone finds my encrypted file?

Without the correct password, the file is mathematically unbreakable. AES-256 encryption would take billions of years to crack with current technology. However, always use strong passwords and secure file storage.

Do you have access to my files?

No. We cannot access your files, passwords, or any data. Everything is processed locally in your browser. We don't have servers that could be compromised or legal requests that could force us to hand over data.
